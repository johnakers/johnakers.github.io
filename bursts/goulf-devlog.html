last update: 2020_03_30

This post is a ever-updated devlog of my foray into game development. I've tinkered in GameMaker in the past but this is my first full attempt to make a non-tinkering full-fledged game.

"Goulf" is one of the original names for golf. I'm trying to make a replica of NES Golf, but be able to customize my own holes and perhaps add a few things or two.

Currently, I'm making a "driving range" mode, that acts as a tutorial. This is also a bit of a tutorial for myself, where a player can hit a ball, reset the postion and hit again. Leveraging Spriters Resource to get assets, I'm currently getting bunkers, trees to be able to customize a specific hole, and in this case a driving range. I believe, Nintendo R&D2 & HAL Laboratory pursued a different way in drawing the entire hole, then based on the location of the ball and its flight were able to programmatically decide what to do (more on this to come, most likely).
